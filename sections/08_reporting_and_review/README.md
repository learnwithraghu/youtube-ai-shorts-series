# Section 8 — Reporting and Review

## Purpose
Create a lightweight way to summarize quality, communicate progress, and review evaluation results over time.

## Section promise
Learners should finish this section able to explain eval results clearly to teammates and stakeholders using summaries, failure buckets, and trends.

## Core teaching goals
- Show why raw scores are not enough on their own
- Summarize evaluation outcomes in a readable way
- Group failures into useful buckets
- Track quality over time
- Close the course with a communication and review workflow

## Main narrative
Once the assistant is being evaluated, compared, and gated, teams need a way to understand what the results mean. Reporting turns technical eval output into something useful for product decisions. This final section shows how to summarize quality, communicate it clearly, and keep improving over time.

## Topics covered
- why reporting matters
- score summaries
- failure buckets
- trend tracking
- stakeholder-friendly review
- course wrap-up and next steps

## What learners should understand
- raw metrics need interpretation
- summaries help teams decide faster
- failure buckets make patterns visible
- trends show whether the system is getting better or worse
- reporting is part of maintaining an eval program

## Section artifact
A lightweight reporting and review workflow for the assistant.
